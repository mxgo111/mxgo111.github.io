About Me

Max is currently a research fellow and pre-doctoral student at Stanford's Graduate School of Business with the Global Capital Allocation Project (GCAP) group. He recently graduated from Harvard University with a joint bachelor's degree in Computer Science and Statistics and a master's degree in Applied Mathematics. In his future career, he hopes to use economic research to inform policy that alleviates long-term extreme poverty, perhaps through working with multilateral institutions like the World Bank and the IMF. He intends to complete an economics Ph.D. in the near future with special interest in economic growth, macroeconomics, or macro-finance.

In his free time, he spends time lifting, playing the piano, juggling, and trying to move up the ranks in Duolingo. [Last Updated October 2023]
