About Me

Max is currently a fourth-year undergraduate student at Harvard University concentrating in Computer Science and Statistics, with a secondary in Economics. He is also an S.M. candidate in Applied Mathematics through Harvard's concurrent master's program. His academic interests vary, including machine learning, artificial intelligence, and development economics. In his future career, he hopes to combine his education in technical AI-related subjects with initiatives from international development to help alleviate extreme poverty*.

Max's experiences include research in machine learning, teaching, quantitative finance, and educational programming. These involve, respectively, collaborations with the CRISP Group and the DtAK lab at Harvard, assistant teaching for several Harvard courses, a trading internship with Five Rings Capital LLC, and conference directing and managing finances for the Harvard Association for U.S. China Relations.

Max also spends time playing the piano, doing calisthenics, lifting, and juggling.

*Currently, the best route he can think of is to pursue higher education, potentially a Ph.D. in economics, and then work at an organization like the World Bank. He welcomes other ideas and would love to connect with anyone thinking about similar things.
